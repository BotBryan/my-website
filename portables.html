PC Requirements

These requirements are for ALL emulators. If you wish to run older systems, the requirements will also be lower.

CPU

Any modern CPU should meet the requirements, though if you are unsure, check its single threaded PassMark Score. You want to aim for at least 1500.

GPU

Graphics performance will relate heavily to the desired resolution. If aiming for 1080p, a GeForce GTX 750, Radeon RX 560, or Intel Arc A380 should be your goal. If aiming for 4k, you will need a much more powerful card, to the tune of a GeForce GTX 1070 Ti.

RAM

You need 8 GB.

Off The Rack Handhelds

Note: Some of these come pre-loaded with ROMs of questionble legality and origin. These devices usually ship from China with cheap, undependable SD cards, so you should buy one from a repuatble manufacturer.

Miyoo Mini V4 (~$60 USD)

The Miyoo Mini is capable of playing all the emulators mentioned on the emulators page, outside of N64, PS2, GameCube and Wii. It is probably the cheapest way to get into portable emulation without sacrificing build quality and usability.

Anbernic RG35XXSP (~$80)

The Anbernic RG35XXSP is pretty similar to the Miyoo, and can play all of the same games, with the benefit of being in a recognizable and useful form factor. It also comes equipped with a Mini HDMI port, so you can connect it to a TV.

Anbernic RG40XX (~$80)

While the same price as the previous handheld, this can also emulate N64 smoothly. You do sacrifice portability, so it should be a decision on if that sacrifice is worth the ability to play N64 games. It retains the Mini HDMI of the previous model.

Anbernic RG406V (~$210)

This device can emulate all systems up to the PS2, at a heavy cost.
